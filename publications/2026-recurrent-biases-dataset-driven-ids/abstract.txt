Intrusion Detection System (IDS) research often relies on benchmark datasets, yet the reasoning behind their selection is prone to error. We apply a 20-item taxonomy of cognitive biases and logical fallacies to IDS studies published between 2020 and 2024. The taxonomy results show that five pitfalls recur consistently: Confirmation Bias (B1), Sampling Bias (B2), Base Rate Fallacy (F1), Cherry Picking (F2), and Hasty Generalization (F3). These appear across studies using NSL-KDD, CICIDS2017, CSE-CIC-IDS2018, UNSW-NB15, CICDDoS2019, and IoT datasets, revealing a pattern of dataset dependence and overstated generalizations. We argue that recognizing these recurrent issues can guide toward improved benchmarking and reporting practices, strengthening the validity of IDS research.
